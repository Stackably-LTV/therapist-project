Cognitive Processing Therapy:

Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT) is an evidence-based treatment for PTSD that helps individuals identify and challenge unhelpful beliefs related to their trauma. Through structured writing and cognitive exercises, CPT supports people in understanding how the trauma has affected their thoughts, emotions, and behaviors—so they can begin to heal and regain a sense of safety and control.

🔹 Core Components of CPT:
Education about PTSD and how thoughts influence recovery


Identifying "Stuck Points"—unhelpful or inaccurate beliefs related to the trauma


Cognitive Restructuring to develop more balanced realistic thinking patterns


Skill Development to support long-term emotional and behavioral change



Homework assignments- To practice cognitive skills and reinforce progress between sessions.

What is the research behind CPT?

Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT) is one of the most extensively researched treatments for post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). It has been shown in numerous clinical trials to significantly reduce PTSD symptoms in military veterans, sexual assault survivors, and individuals who have experienced a wide range of traumas. CPT is recognized as a gold-standard, evidence-based treatment by organizations such as the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, the Department of Defense, and the American Psychological Association. Research has also demonstrated that CPT can lead to long-term improvements in emotional functioning, relationships, and overall quality of life. Learn more here and here.




Prolonged Exposure Therapy:

PE helps individuals gradually confront trauma-related memories, feelings, and situations they’ve been avoiding, so that distress decreases over time and trauma no longer controls their life. By facing what feels unsafe in a structured, supported way, people can reclaim a sense of safety and control.

Psychoeducation about trauma, PTSD, and how avoidance maintains symptoms


Imaginal Exposure: Repeatedly recounting the trauma memory in a safe, therapeutic setting


In Vivo Exposure: Gradually confronting safe but avoided situations in real life


Processing: Reflecting on the exposure experience to make meaning and reduce fear


Homework Assignments to practice exposures and reinforce progress between sessions

Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP):

ERP involves gradually facing feared thoughts, images, or situations (exposures) while resisting the urge to engage in compulsive behaviors (response prevention). Over time, this helps reduce anxiety and retrains the brain to respond in healthier, more adaptive ways.

Exposure to feared thoughts, situations, or triggers in a gradual, structured way


Response Prevention by resisting compulsions or rituals used to reduce anxiety


Hierarchy Development to rank triggers from least to most distressing


Repetition and Practice to build tolerance and reduce fear responses


Guided Support to process emotions and reinforce progress during and between sessions

What is the research behind ERP?

Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP) is widely considered the gold-standard treatment for Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD). Decades of research, including numerous randomized controlled trials, have consistently shown that ERP significantly reduces OCD symptoms and improves overall functioning. Studies have demonstrated that ERP is effective for both adults and children, and its benefits are often long-lasting when practiced consistently. Leading organizations such as the American Psychological Association, the International OCD Foundation, and the National Institute of Mental Health all strongly endorse ERP as a first-line treatment for OCD. CRAFT:

CRAFT is an evidence-based approach that helps concerned family members or loved ones support someone struggling with substance use—without using confrontation or ultimatums. CRAFT teaches practical strategies to improve communication, reinforce positive behavior, and increase the chances that their loved one will seek treatment, all while improving the well-being of the family member themselves.



Positive Communication Skills to reduce conflict and build connection


Reinforcement of Sober Behavior through natural rewards and support


Allowing Natural Consequences instead of enabling substance use


Self-Care and Life Enrichment for the concerned loved one’s emotional health


Treatment Invitation Strategies that increase the likelihood of engagement without pressure

What is the research behind CRAFT?

CRAFT is a well-researched, evidence-based intervention that has been shown to effectively help concerned family members support a loved one with substance use issues. Studies consistently demonstrate that CRAFT is more effective than traditional methods like confrontation or enabling, with research showing that up to 64–74% of individuals whose family members use CRAFT enter treatment—significantly higher than other approaches. CRAFT not only increases treatment engagement but also improves the emotional well-being and quality of life of the concerned loved one. It is endorsed by the National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA) and included in SAMHSA’s National Registry of Evidence-based Programs and Practices. Learn more here and here.
